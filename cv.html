2007, Computer Science PhD, University of Southampton.

This involved simulations of a great many very small devices, each about the size of a grain of sand, and each capable of local broadcast communications with a few nearby neighbouring devices. If such devices could be mass produced very cheaply, they could conceivably be mixed and suspended in paint, then painted over a large surface such as a bridge or ceiling.

The challenge of such large scale computing is to reliably obtain a desired behaviour by engineering the cooperation of many parts, without assuming any precision interconnect or precision arrangement of the parts.

One of the main contributions of the thesis was a novel distributed algorithm to detect the perimeter of such a large scale network without requiring nodes to have any knowledge of their location.
